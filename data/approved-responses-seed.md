**Q:** My friend wants a primer on using AI for his work in beverage sales. What resources or approach would you recommend to help him get started?

**Sent:** I would start by looking at his workflow and identifying repetitive, busy work tasks. He probably has to prepare whenever he calls or visits customers/prospects. Maybe set him up with a skill that helps him do background research for each customer. Or after he does a call with a customer, take the transcript, identify the action items, draft an email that he can send to all recipients, and interface with his CRM to track those same action items. Or maybe a skill with a good prompt for drafting decks. Or meeting planning. Basically, help him with his busy work so he can focus on higher level thinking. This might involve linking Cowork to an MCP server for his CRM system or his email/calendar. I would ask Claude to help you help him with all of this. Tell it who you are and what you're trying to do, and describe who you're doing it for, and ask it to interview you about how best to help your friend.

**Q:** Do y'all have recommendations for prompts? My experience is limited to M365 Copilot and a little ChatGPT — really basic stuff. Examples or websites would be helpful.

**Sent:** My suggestion in how to prompt is to ask ChatGPT something like: "I am a novice user of LLMs. You are my expert teacher who can help me get up to speed with prompt writing. Slowly walk me through setting an effective prompt for <whatever you want to find out>." Have it tell you why it's making the suggestions it's making. For big system prompts, I ask an LLM to write it for me and then I review it. And try to challenge yourself to use it once daily for something, anything. Pretty soon you'll be thinking AI-first.
